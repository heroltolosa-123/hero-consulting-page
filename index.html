Why a statistician, not a formatter

Most dissertation help stops at grammar and formatting. My work starts where that ends — at whether your sampling design holds up, whether your model specification matches your hypotheses, and whether your results section will survive a panel that knows what to ask. I know what that panel is looking for, because I've sat on that side of the table too, evaluating research defenses at PUP.

I've built credit risk models at a listed bank, forecasting models at Accenture, and taught graduate statistics for six years at PUP. That range is what lets me sit across from a physician doing an IPA study one week, and an executive running a mixed-methods dissertation the next — and speak both languages: the discipline's and the data's.

Clients are typically physicians, senior executives, and working professionals completing a Master's or Doctorate while holding down a full-time career. The engagement is built around that reality.
